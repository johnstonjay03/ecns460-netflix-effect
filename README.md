# ecns460-netflix-effect
1. Topic: The "Netflix Effect" on Local Real Estate
Our project looks at whether popular TV shows and movies filmed in certain places have an impact on local housing markets and short-term rentals. For example, shows like Yellowstone in Montana or The White Lotus in Sicily brought a lot of attention to those locations. We want to see if that attention leads to higher housing prices or higher Zillow rental rates in those areas.
2. Analysis Plan 
Research Question: Does the release of a popular TV series or movie filmed in a specific location cause a statistically significant increase in local housing price indices or short-term rental premiums compared to similar non-filming locations? We plan to conduct a Causal Analysis using a Difference-in-Differences (DiD) framework.
Treatment Group: Counties or cities where major productions were filmed and released during our sample period.
Control Group: "Synthetic" or similar peer cities/counties that share similar pre-treatment economic trends but did not host a major production.
The Shock: The release date of the show/movie.
3. The Challenge
One of the main challenges is collecting and constructing a dataset that links entertainment media releases with local economic data. Because housing prices and short-term rental datasets have to be downloaded for specific cities or countries, we must first identify the shows and filming locations that will be included in our analysis. This requires manually identifying filming locations, determining the appropriate geographic units (city, state, country, etc), and aligning those locations with available housing price and rental data. Additionally, assembling out data together will require scraping and merging multiple raw data sources. This will require a lot of preparation before any analysis can begin. 
4. Data Sources
Source 1: IMDb Public Datasets https://datasets.imdbws.com/ 
IMDb provides structured metadata on television titles, including title identifiers, title type, and release year. We will use the ‘title.basics’ dataset to identify television series and assign a release year to each show. Example variables include tconst (unique identifier for each title), primaryTitle (name of the show), titleType (types of production), startYear (year show first aired), and endYear (final year of the series, if applicable). 
IMDb dataset includes titles from early film history (some as early as 1880) to present. For this project we will focus on television series released approximately between 2000 and present. IMDb data spans over multiple geographic regions, global and international, this global metadata will be matched with filming location obtained from Wikipedia. 
Source 2: Wikipedia https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_films_shot_in_New_Mexico (example URL since we will use multiple depending on the city we are looking for)
Wikipedia provides show pages, category pages, and production-list pages that identify where shows were filmed. We will scrape these pages to get filming locations plus potentially bigger lists of shows filmed in major production cities/counties that IMDb may not provide. This source provides the geographic treatment location that IMDb does not always include. The wikipedia dataset provides the geographic filming locations for shows and movies. This will allow us to identify specific cities, countries, and regions to be used for treatment areas in the DiD.
Source 3: FHFA House Price Index (HPI) https://www.fhfa.gov/data/hpi/datasets 
The FHFA datasets provide county-level housing price index data over time. We will use the annual county-level dataset as our main housing price outcome variable to measure country housing market changes before and after show release. Example variables include county (county name), state (State name), year (year of observation), HPI (House Price Index value), fips_code (County identifier). 
The HPI dataset spans approximately 1975 to the present. For this will focus on 2000 to present to match the period of television show release. This dataset covers counties across the United States. 
Source 4: Zillow Research Data https://www.zillow.com/research/data/ 
Zillow will provide our county-level rental market measures. We will use Zillow as a second market outcome source so we can compare both housing-price and rental price effects at the county level. Zillow’s public datasets typically cover a timespan from the late 1990s to present across national, state, and county levels in the U.S. 
IMDb will provide the list of television shows and their release year. Wikipedia scraping will provide filming locations, plus additional shows filmed in specific places. Those filming locations will be standardized and mapped to counties. The county and release-year information will then be merged with the FHFA county-year housing data and Zillow county-level market data to create the final county-year panel for analysis.
